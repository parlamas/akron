Invitations, guests and parties (p. 5)

Task (in class / in groups). Customs & Traditions

  1. You are invited for dinner at 18:00 to a Danish family. What time do you arrive?

    Before 18:00 / At 18:00 / After 18:00 (What time?)

    What do you do in your country? What traditions do you* have?

  2. Do you bring something fot the host / hostess? If yes, what?

    What do you do in your country? What traditions do you have?

Task 2. Specific expression. What do you say in these situations?

  1. You are having guests for dinner. The guests knock on the door and you open. What do you say?

  2. The guests give you a bouquet of flowers / a bottle of wine. What do you say? What do they answer?

  3. You are for dinner at a friend's place. You need the salt. What do you say?

  4. You are on a visit at a friend's place. You* are done with the dinner. What do you say? What does your friend say?

  5. You have visited a friend. Itøs late, and you must go. What do you say?

  6. A couple of days later, you run into your friend on the street. What do you say? How does he respond?
